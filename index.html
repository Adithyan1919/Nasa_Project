üîÑ How It Works: The Complete Process

1
Collection Robots
Autonomous "Pollen" units patrol the habitat, identifying and collecting waste using cameras and NIR sensors. They perform initial shredding (5-20mm) and compression, then deliver materials to the airlock with dust-resistant actuators and swarm communication.
2
Airlock Intake
Sealed interface between external robots and pressurized micro-hub. Features mechanical seals, air recovery systems, particle traps, and chemical assay stations to prevent Martian dust ingress and isolate chemical hazards.
3
High-Fidelity Sorting
Advanced separation using eddy-current technology for metals, optical/NIR sensors for polymers (PE, PET, Nylon), and density/air separators for foams. Ensures 95%+ sorting accuracy for optimal recycling efficiency.
4
Preprocessing
Shredders, granulators, and dryers create controlled feedstock sizes. HEPA and charged filters capture microfibers. Materials are cleaned with minimal water in a closed-loop system integrated with habitat ECLSS.
5
Material Processing
Polymers extruded into pellets/filaments with inline composition verification. Metals undergo cold-press straightening and friction-stir joining. Optional low-oxygen thermal cell for pyrolysis of mixed plastics with full scrubbing.
6
Product Manufacturing
Additive manufacturing bay with FDM/pellet extruders produces on-demand utensils, storage bins, and small tools. Thermoforming and ultrasonic welding create panels. Regolith press makes composite bricks using waste polymers and Martian soil.
7
AI Management
Waste Intelligence system provides material traceability, optimizes fleet routing, generates predictive sorting rules, schedules maintenance, and performs product quality checks. Reduces crew workload by 80%.
8
Safe Storage
Hazard vault quarantines toxic materials, unknown substances, and electronics waste. Prevents PFAS and microplastic release. Stores materials for potential Earth return or future advanced processing capabilities.
